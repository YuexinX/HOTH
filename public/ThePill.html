What is the Pill?

The Pill refers to oral contraceptives which contains hormones that when taken daily by mouth at the same time prevents pregnancy. It contains hormones that inhibit ovulation, thicken cervical mucus to impede sperm movement, and alter the uterine lining to make implantation less likely. These are considered short acting and if you can stop using them at any time if you want to get pregnant. There are two main types of pills combined oral contraceptivesand Progestin-only pills. When used properly the pill is 93% effective

Combined Oral Contraceptives

Combined oral contraceptives or COCs contain both estrogen and a progestin to stop the ovaries from releasing eggs. Common side effects include spotting or bleeding between periods, Nausea, Breast tenderness, and headaches. These are the most common type of birth control.

Progestin-only pills

Progestin-only pills or POPs or mini pills only have progestin. It thickens the cervical mucus, which keeps sperm from getting to the egg. Common side effects include Irregular bleeding, Nausea, Breast tenderness, and Headache
