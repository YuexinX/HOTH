What is an IUD?

An IUD or intrauterine device is a T-shaped contraceptive device inserted into the uterus to prevent pregnancy.There are two main types: hormonal IUDs, which release a hormone to prevent pregnancy, and copper IUDs, which use copper to create an environment that is toxic to sperm, thus preventing fertilization. IUDs are highly effective, long-lasting forms of birth control that can be easily removed when desired. They offer a convenient and reversible option for individuals seeking reliable contraception.
