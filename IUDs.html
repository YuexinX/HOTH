What is an IUD?

An IUD or intrauterine device is a T-shaped contraceptive device inserted into the uterus to prevent pregnancy.There are two main types: hormonal IUDs, which release a hormone to prevent pregnancy, and copper IUDs, which use copper to create an environment that is toxic to sperm, thus preventing fertilization. IUDs are highly effective, long-lasting forms of birth control that can be easily removed when desired. They offer a convenient and reversible option for individuals seeking reliable contraception. It is 99% effective.

Hormonal IUD

Hormonal IUDs like Mirena, Kyleena, Liletta, and Skyla. use the hormone progestin to prevent pregnancy. Progestin is very similar to the hormone progesterone that our bodies make naturally. Mirena works for up to 8 years. Kyleena works for up to 5 years. Liletta works for up to 8 years. Skyla works for up to 3 years.

Copper IUD

Copper IUDs like Paragard is a wrapped in a tiny bit of copper, and it protects you from pregnancy for up to 12 years.
